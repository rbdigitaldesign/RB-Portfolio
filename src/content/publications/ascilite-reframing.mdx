### Concise summary of the submission draft

-   **Problem framing:** Course development in Australian universities is often treated as a complicated, linear process; in reality it is a complex endeavour shaped by competing priorities (quality, accreditation, market pressures, diverse stakeholders).
-   **Proposition:** Reframe development as a complex, adaptive, collaborative practice informed by design thinking and systems thinking.
-   **Six guiding principles:**
    1.  Establish shared values and language for scope clarity
    2.  Prioritise team-centred collaboration across roles
    3.  Keep processes human-centred
    4.  Embrace iteration and continuous improvement
    5.  Use collaborative tools and visual artefacts
    6.  Work across organisational layers to align strategy and delivery
-   **Notable innovations:** Visual course maps; program workshops; a smart storyboard tool; a shared vocabulary of learning types/patterns.
-   **Outcomes:** 36 courses and supporting digital resources delivered; the greater impact was embedding adaptive, design-led practice that builds institutional capacity and shifts the target from MVP to Maximum Value.
-   **Implication:** Universities can better meet uncertainty by adopting scalable, collaborative, design-informed approaches.
