### concise summary of the submission draft

#### problem framing

course development in australian universities is often treated as a complicated, linear process; in reality it is a complex endeavour shaped by competing priorities (quality, accreditation, market pressures, diverse stakeholders).

#### proposition

reframe development as a complex, adaptive, collaborative practice informed by design thinking and systems thinking.

#### six guiding principles

1.  establish shared values and language for scope clarity
2.  prioritise team-centred collaboration across roles
3.  keep processes human-centred
4.  embrace iteration and continuous improvement
5.  use collaborative tools and visual artefacts
6.  work across organisational layers to align strategy and delivery

#### notable innovations

visual course maps; program workshops; a smart storyboard tool; a shared vocabulary of learning types/patterns.

#### outcomes

36 courses and supporting digital resources delivered; the greater impact was embedding adaptive, design-led practice that builds institutional capacity and shifts the target from mvp to maximum value.

#### implication

universities can better meet uncertainty by adopting scalable, collaborative, design-informed approaches.
